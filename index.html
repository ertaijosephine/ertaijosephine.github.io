Ertai's story

When we first met at the beginning of our studies at Cornell University, her kindness, intelligence, and beauty immediately caught my attention. I perceived her as friendly and easygoing, and we gradually began to enjoy our time together, including many many hotpot meals. As our companionship deepened over time, my feelings for her evolved into genuine fondness. I believed that she reciprocated my affection, given her consistent willingness to spend time with me. Yet, I eventually came to realize that her enthusiasm was primarily centered around hotpots themselves, not directed towards me personally. This misunderstanding in addition to some life problems brought some turbulence in our relationship, leading to separation for some time. Amidst the isolation and challenges brought about by the unique circumstances of the COVID era, I found myself reflecting on our past conversations about Christianity. I came to understand that I do not own my life, and memories of Josephine attributing her accomplishments to the Lord while maintaining faith in struggles kept returning to me. I started reading the books she recommended and developed a new understanding about the world, even though I knew that there would be no more stories between us. However, the Lord had different plans. Our paths unexpectedly reconnected while helping each other during the COVID quarantine. Slowly, we revisited the past and our hearts drew closer. She introduced me to her church community, pastor, and loved ones. Through patient yet resolute discussions and debates, they nurtured and fortified my faith. Our relationship paralleled the growth of my spirituality, an evolution that continues to this day. I firmly believe that our story is a living miracle of the Lord, who challenges us to the righteousness, guides us in the wilderness, and silently protects us throughout our journey toward Him.

Josephine's story:

We first met at Cornell University in 2018 and began to enjoy each other's companionship. At that time Ertai was an atheist, so I just saw him as a cherished friend rather than a potential partner. I know that he has been interested and has been exploring Christianity for several years, and he also asked (and debated) me many questions about Christianity during our interaction. Many of his questions were very deep, ranging from the nature of suffering to profound theological matters. I connected him to my brother Johan and referred him to a church & several books. Life's difficult challenges often draw us closer to God, a truth Ertai experienced firsthand. In his darkest moments, he found God to be the only logical explanation to many of the questions he has. Ertai later gave himself to Jesus and got baptized. He even now co-leads the bible study at our church in the US, an accomplishment that I am most proud of. Ertai's qualities resonate deeply with me. His genuine kindness, extended not just to me but to everyone, is a rarity. Sincerity defines him; he remains authentic, unswayed by facades. This kindness and authenticity made him very endeared to my family. Ertai's family mirrors his warmth and kindness, especially his parents. Our journey together hasn't been without challenges. We faced many hardships and challenges together. But through it all, we became better in communicating, loving, and understanding each other. Together, we also experienced spiritual growth with God. All of these made me certain that Ertai Liu is the partner whom God has brought for me.

Thank you!
